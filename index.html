The late 1700s of Canada

Era of the Loyalists

December.16, 1773

The Boston Tea Party was a protest held by American colonists, against the British Parliament for the imposed tax they had to pay. This tax was to gather money to pay the debt Britain was under. Britain thought the taxes for this were quite fair, but the American colonists didn't agree with this and didn't want to be taxed. This is what the whole protest was about. To be exact, in this protest Americans dressed up as “native Indians” (indigenous people) and got on colonist ships to throw Britain’s valuable tea into the water. This then led to Britain sending in their armies with full force.

1774

The Cumberland house opened in Saskatchewan and was Hudson’s Bay's first inland trading house. This tiny hut was built to mainly store gunpowder, but it was still one of the most important trading posts for the Hudson's Bay Company. This is because it was surrounded by trade communities, French and English to the east, Athabasca County to the north, and Oregon County to the west.

June.22, 1774

On June 22nd, 1774 the Quebec act was passed, which aimed to gain the loyalty of the French Canadians. This act stated to restore the rights of the French. It was a great rule, although it is said it later became the reason for the American Revolutionary War.

May.10, 1775

In 1775, American revolutionary forces planned on capturing British controlled Quebec and bringing French-canadiens (The name Canada was used but not yet established) on the side of the Patriots and fighting for freedom from the British.

July.4, 1776

By the year 1776, the Patiriots had control over the 13 colonies, and the supporters of the crown could no longer stay. So many of the loyalists fled from America to Canada. A few loyalists didn't establish their support of the crown allowing them to stay and later participate in the American revolution.

1779 - 1783

After taking over New France, in the years 1779 - 1783 Britian established a fur trading platform known as The North West Company. This was the competive company to the Hudson's Bay Company, whcich also aimed to trade fur for valuable resources.

May.19, 1780

This unusual event occured when the sky appeared to darken during afternoon hours. The darkness is assumed to be smoke from forest fires, in addition to thick fog and terrible clime in the region. Letters wrote in that time period from revolutionary soldiers to common civilians, point to the fact that heave cloud cover also produced a certain colour in the sky. Unlike now, when we have scientific technologies to predict what and why the sky darkened, people in the 1780s lacked these tools and interpreted it in religious terms; saying that judgement day had arrived or was soon to come (Judgement day is a religous term used in Christianity to refer o the end of the world when god decides everybody's fate).

May.18, 1783

After a few years, in 1783 the second wave of loytalists arrive in Canada. They come as refuges of the British crown, because at that time Canada was also a part of Britain.

Sept.3, 1783

The treaty of paris was what officially recognized the independence of the United States of America from British power. This also meant a border between the British controlled regions and America, namely a border between British North America and the USA. The border was decided to run through the Great Lakes up until what they thought was the end of the Mississippi River. In addition to this, indigenous peoples in the American territory were given reserves and fishing rights for water bodies near Newfoundland were given to the Americans.

August.16, 1784

In 1784, Nova scota was divided making another colony, by the descision of the British Government. The divided part is now known as New Brunswik.

October.25, 1784

The Haldimand Proclamation was a rule ordered by the governor of Quebec, which was to give land on either side of the Grand river to the 6 nations of the Haudenosaunee. This was in recognition of their alliance with Britain in the American revolution, since the loss in the civil war had caused them to lose their land. After the war had ended, many Mohawk leaders pressured the British government into making land reserves for their people.

June.3, 1789

In the year 1789, Alexander Mackenzie set out from Fort Chipewyan to find the Pacific with a small group to study the geography and plan new trade routes. Throughout the journey, he and his group were put through severe conditions, and overall a really hard journey. They surprisingly traveled a long way in just 3 months, and were doing good. Unfortunately though, the group wasn't able to reach the Pacific Ocean, but at least learnt more about Canada's northwestern geography.

June.10, 1791

The Constitutional Act of 1791 which is also known as the Canada Act, was an act by the British Parliament. This act split Quebec into two parts, which were Upper Canada(Ontario) and Lower Canada(Québec).

1795

In 1795's a fur trading company was founded by some group of men, known as the XY company. This company was in direct competion with the NorthWest Company, and was the reason it was named the two letters after W, which were X and Y.
